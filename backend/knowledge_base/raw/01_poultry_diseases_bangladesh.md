# Common Poultry Diseases in Bangladesh — Identification, Treatment & Prevention

## 1. Newcastle Disease (রাণীক্ষেত রোগ / Ranikhet)

### Overview
Newcastle Disease (ND) is one of the most devastating viral diseases affecting poultry in Bangladesh. It is caused by Avian Paramyxovirus Type 1 (APMV-1). The disease is significantly more prevalent in layer chickens (27.4%) than broiler chickens (6.8%). It is locally known as "Ranikhet" or "রাণীক্ষেত রোগ".

### Symptoms
- Sudden high mortality (can reach 100% in unvaccinated flocks)
- Greenish watery diarrhea (সবুজ পাতলা পায়খানা)
- Twisting of neck (torticollis / ঘাড় বেঁকে যাওয়া)
- Respiratory distress, gasping, coughing (শ্বাসকষ্ট)
- Drop in egg production (ডিম উৎপাদন কমে যাওয়া)
- Soft-shelled or misshapen eggs
- Swollen eyes (চোখ ফোলা)
- Loss of appetite (খাবার বন্ধ করা)
- Paralysis of legs and wings
- Trembling

### Treatment
- **No specific antiviral treatment exists** for Newcastle Disease.
- Treatment is supportive only:
  - Electrolyte supplementation in drinking water
  - Vitamins A, E, and C supplementation
  - Antibiotics for secondary bacterial infections (e.g., Amoxicillin 10mg/kg body weight for 3-5 days)
- **Critical Action**: Contact the nearest Upazila Livestock Office (উপজেলা প্রাণিসম্পদ অফিস) immediately.
- Isolate sick birds from healthy flock.
- Do not sell or transport sick birds.

### Prevention
- **Vaccination is the primary prevention**:
  - Day 3-5: BCRDV (Baby Chick Ranikhet Disease Vaccine) — 1 drop in eye
  - Day 21-24: Bangla ND LaSota — eye drop or drinking water
  - Week 8-10: LaSota booster
  - Week 16-18: Killed ND vaccine (RDV) — injection for layers before laying
- Practice strict biosecurity: restrict visitor access, disinfect footwear
- Maintain clean water and feed sources

### Withdrawal Period
- Not applicable (no antiviral treatment)
- For antibiotics used against secondary infections: follow specific drug withdrawal period

## 2. Avian Influenza / Bird Flu (বার্ড ফ্লু)

### Overview
Avian Influenza (AI) is a highly pathogenic viral disease. Both High Pathogenicity (HP) H5N1 and Low Pathogenicity (LP) H9N2 strains are currently **endemic in poultry in Bangladesh**. AI is more common in layer chickens (10.7%) than broilers (1.4%). This is a **notifiable disease** — any suspected case MUST be reported to authorities.

### Symptoms
- Sudden death without prior symptoms (কোনো লক্ষণ ছাড়াই হঠাৎ মৃত্যু)
- Swollen head, comb, and wattles (মাথা, ঝুঁটি ও কানের লতি ফোলা)
- Purple/blue discoloration of comb and wattles (ঝুঁটি বেগুনি হয়ে যাওয়া)
- Severe drop in egg production
- Watery diarrhea
- Respiratory signs: coughing, sneezing, nasal discharge
- Lack of energy, huddling
- Edema of face and head
- Hemorrhages on legs and feet

### Treatment
- **No treatment is recommended or available.**
- **MANDATORY ACTION**: Report immediately to:
  - Nearest Upazila Livestock Office
  - District Veterinary Hospital
  - DLS Emergency Hotline
- Cull affected and exposed birds as per government protocol
- Dispose of dead birds safely (burial or incineration, NOT selling)

### Prevention
- **Vaccination**: Some farms use AI vaccines, but this is regulated by DLS
- **Strict biosecurity** is the primary defense:
  - Restrict entry of wild birds into poultry sheds
  - Do not allow visitors from other farms
  - Change clothes and disinfect footwear before entering shed
  - Separate equipment for each shed
  - Avoid contact with live bird markets
  - Do not mix species (ducks, geese with chickens)
- Report any unusual mortality to authorities within 24 hours

### Zoonotic Risk
- H5N1 can infect humans. Farmers should wear gloves and masks when handling sick birds.
- Cook poultry meat thoroughly (>70°C throughout)
- Eggs should be fully cooked, not consumed raw

## 3. Infectious Bursal Disease / Gumboro (গামবোরো)

### Overview
Gumboro disease targets the immune system (bursa of Fabricius) of young chickens, making them susceptible to other infections. Young chicks (0-6 weeks) are most vulnerable. Morbidity can reach 100% and mortality 80-90% in severe cases.

### Symptoms
- Whitish or watery diarrhea (সাদা পাতলা পায়খানা)
- Ruffled feathers (পালক উসকো খুসকো)
- Depression and huddling
- Trembling
- Loss of appetite
- Vent pecking (self-mutilation around vent)
- Dehydration
- Swollen and hemorrhagic bursa (post-mortem finding)

### Treatment
- No specific antiviral treatment
- Supportive care:
  - Electrolyte solution in water: mix 1 teaspoon salt + 1 tablespoon sugar per liter of water
  - Vitamin supplementation (Vitamin A, D3, E, K)
  - Keep birds warm and comfortable
  - Antibiotics if secondary infection: Enrofloxacin 10mg/kg for 3-5 days
- **Withdrawal period for Enrofloxacin**: 7-10 days for meat

### Prevention
- **Vaccination** (critical):
  - Day 10-14: Live IBD vaccine (intermediate strain) — eye drop or drinking water
  - Day 18-21: IBD booster — drinking water
  - In high-risk areas: additional booster at day 28
- Maintain maternal antibody levels in breeder flocks
- Practice good hygiene: clean and disinfect sheds between flocks

## 4. Coccidiosis (ককসিডিওসিস)

### Overview
Coccidiosis is caused by protozoan parasites of the genus Eimeria. It is extremely common in Bangladesh, especially in broiler flocks during the rainy/monsoon season (June-September) when litter moisture is high. Multiple species exist: E. tenella (cecal), E. acervulina (intestinal), E. maxima, E. necatrix.

### Symptoms
- **Cecal coccidiosis (E. tenella)**:
  - Bloody droppings (রক্তমিশ্রিত পায়খানা)
  - High mortality in young birds
  - Severe anemia, pale comb
- **Intestinal coccidiosis (E. acervulina)**:
  - Watery or mucoid droppings
  - Poor feed conversion
  - Reduced growth rate
- **Common signs**:
  - Ruffled feathers
  - Decreased feed intake (খাওয়া কমে যাওয়া)
  - Weight loss / poor growth (ওজন কমা)
  - Pale comb and wattles (ঝুঁটি ফ্যাকাশে)
  - Huddling with droopy wings
  - Dehydration

### Treatment
- **Amprolium (Amprol/Coxiprol)**: 
  - Dose: 250mg per liter of drinking water for 5-7 days
  - Or: 1.25g per kg of feed for 5-7 days
  - **Withdrawal period: 1 day for meat, 0 days for eggs**
- **Toltrazuril (Baycox/Coxinil)**:
  - Dose: 7mg per kg body weight, single dose (25ml of 2.5% solution per liter of drinking water for 2 days)
  - **Withdrawal period: 14 days for meat**
- **Sulfonamides (Sulfadimethoxine)**:
  - Dose: 50mg per kg body weight for 5 days
  - **Withdrawal period: 5 days for meat, 10 days for eggs**
- Supportive: Vitamin K (helps control bleeding), electrolytes

### Prevention
- Keep litter dry (below 25-30% moisture)
- Anticoccidial in feed (preventive programs for broilers):
  - Salinomycin or Monensin in feed
  - Shuttle program: rotate drugs each flock cycle
- Avoid overcrowding
- Clean and disinfect sheds between batches
- In monsoon: increase ventilation, reduce litter moisture, stir litter daily

## 5. Infectious Bronchitis (সংক্রামক ব্রংকাইটিস / IB)

### Overview
Infectious Bronchitis is a highly contagious respiratory disease caused by a coronavirus. It affects both broilers and layers. In layers, it significantly reduces egg production and causes poor egg quality.

### Symptoms
- Sneezing and coughing (হাঁচি ও কাশি)
- Nasal discharge (নাক দিয়ে পানি পড়া)
- Wet eyes / tearing (চোখ ভেজা)
- Reduced egg production (ডিম কমে যাওয়া)
- Misshapen, rough, or soft-shelled eggs
- Gasping in young chicks (বাচ্চা মুরগির শ্বাসকষ্ট)
- Rales (abnormal breathing sounds)
- In severe cases: kidney damage, increased water consumption

### Treatment
- **No specific antiviral treatment**
- Supportive care:
  - Electrolytes in drinking water
  - Vitamin C (200-300mg per liter of water)
  - Antibiotics to prevent secondary bacterial infections
  - Brooding temperature increase by 2-3°C for chicks
  - Reduce stress (minimize handling, reduce stocking density)
- Recovery takes 10-14 days; egg production may not fully recover in layers

### Prevention
- **Vaccination**:
  - Day 5-7: Live IB vaccine (H120 strain) — eye drop or spray
  - Week 6-8: IB booster
  - Week 16-18: Killed IB vaccine for layers (before laying)
- Good ventilation to reduce ammonia levels
- Maintain biosecurity: limit visitors, disinfect equipment

## 6. Fowl Pox (মুরগির বসন্ত / Fowl Pox)

### Overview
Fowl Pox is caused by Avipoxvirus. It spreads through mosquito bites (common during monsoon), direct contact, or contaminated equipment. Two forms exist: dry pox (cutaneous) and wet pox (diphtheritic).

### Symptoms
- **Dry Pox (শুকনো পক্স)**:
  - Wart-like nodules on comb, wattles, face, and eyelids (ঝুঁটি, কানের লতি, মুখে আঁচিলের মতো ক্ষত)
  - Scabs on unfeathered skin
  - Mild form, birds usually recover
- **Wet Pox (ভেজা পক্স)**:
  - Yellowish lesions in mouth, throat, and trachea (মুখ/গলায় ক্ষত)
  - Difficulty breathing (শ্বাসকষ্ট)
  - Difficulty eating and drinking
  - Higher mortality than dry pox
- Reduced egg production
- Weight loss

### Treatment
- **No specific antiviral treatment**
- Supportive care:
  - Clean lesions with dilute iodine solution (Povidone-iodine)
  - Apply glycerin on dry pox lesions
  - For wet pox: remove diphtheritic membranes gently, apply iodine
  - Vitamin A supplementation (helps tissue repair): 10,000 IU per liter of water for 5 days
  - Antibiotics for secondary infections: Oxytetracycline 20mg/kg for 5 days
  - **Withdrawal period for Oxytetracycline: 7 days for meat, 5 days for eggs**

### Prevention
- **Vaccination**: Fowl Pox vaccine at week 4-6 — wing-web puncture method
  - One dose provides long-lasting immunity
- Mosquito control: use mosquito nets, drain stagnant water near shed
- Keep sheds clean and well-ventilated

## 7. Marek's Disease (ম্যারেক্স রোগ)

### Overview
Marek's Disease is caused by a herpesvirus. It primarily affects young chickens between 8-20 weeks of age. The virus spreads through feather dander and dust. It causes tumors, paralysis, and immunosuppression.

### Symptoms
- Paralysis of legs and wings (পা/ডানা প্যারালাইসিস) — classic form
- One leg forward, one backward ("splits" position)
- Weight loss despite eating
- Grey iris / irregular pupil (ocular form) (চোখের মণি ধূসর)
- Skin tumors (cutaneous form)
- Enlarged nerves (sciatic, brachial)
- Mortality in young birds (8-20 weeks)
- In visceral form: tumors in liver, spleen, kidney, gonads

### Treatment
- **No treatment available**
- Affected birds should be culled humanely
- Focus on prevention through vaccination

### Prevention
- **Vaccination at Day 1 at hatchery**: HVT (Herpesvirus of Turkey) vaccine — subcutaneous injection
  - This is the single most important vaccine in commercial poultry
  - Must be given within 24 hours of hatch
- Maintain good biosecurity
- All-in, all-out flock management
- Clean and disinfect shed thoroughly between batches
- Allow 2-3 weeks rest period between batches

## 8. Mycoplasmosis / Chronic Respiratory Disease (CRD) (সিআরডি)

### Overview
Caused by Mycoplasma gallisepticum (MG) and Mycoplasma synoviae (MS). Very common in Bangladesh, especially during winter (November-February) and in poorly ventilated sheds. Often occurs as a secondary infection with other respiratory diseases.

### Symptoms
- Sneezing and nasal discharge (হাঁচি ও নাক থেকে পানি পড়া)
- Swollen sinuses (infraorbital) — "পাফি ফেস"
- Frothy eyes
- Reduced feed intake
- Drop in egg production
- Rattling/gurgling breathing sounds
- In M. synoviae: swollen joints, lameness

### Treatment
- **Tylosin (Tylox/Tylan)**:
  - Dose: 0.5g per liter of drinking water for 3-5 days
  - **Withdrawal period: 5 days for meat, 3 days for eggs**
- **Doxycycline**:
  - Dose: 10-20mg per kg body weight for 5-7 days
  - **Withdrawal period: 5 days for meat, 7 days for eggs**
- **Tiamulin (Tiamutin)**:
  - Dose: 0.025% in drinking water for 3-5 days
  - **Withdrawal period: 7 days for meat**
- Improve ventilation in shed
- Reduce ammonia levels (stirr litter, reduce moisture)

### Prevention
- MG vaccination at week 6-8 (live vaccine via eye drop)
- Maintain good ventilation — ammonia levels below 25ppm
- Avoid overcrowding
- Practice all-in, all-out management
- Clean and disinfect between flocks

## 9. Colibacillosis (E. coli Infection / কলিব্যাসিলোসিস)

### Overview
Caused by pathogenic Escherichia coli. One of the most common bacterial diseases in Bangladesh poultry. Often occurs as secondary infection after viral respiratory diseases. Poor sanitation and contaminated water are major risk factors.

### Symptoms
- Airsacculitis (inflammation of air sacs)
- Perihepatitis (inflammation around liver)
- Pericarditis (inflammation of heart sac)
- Swollen joints (arthritis)
- Egg peritonitis in layers
- Omphalitis (yolk sac infection in chicks) — "নাভি ফুলে যাওয়া"
- Diarrhea, depression, ruffled feathers

### Treatment
- **Colistin**:
  - Dose: 75,000-100,000 IU per kg body weight for 3-5 days in drinking water
  - **Withdrawal period: 7 days for meat, 0 days for eggs**
  - **Note**: Colistin is a last-resort antibiotic. Use judiciously.
- **Ciprofloxacin**:
  - Dose: 10mg per kg body weight for 3-5 days
  - **Withdrawal period: 10 days for meat, 3 days for eggs**
- **Amoxicillin**:
  - Dose: 10-20mg per kg body weight for 3-5 days
  - **Withdrawal period: 7 days for meat, 0 days for eggs**
- Culture and sensitivity testing recommended before choosing antibiotic

### Prevention
- Clean water supply (chlorinate: 2-3 ppm free chlorine)
- Good sanitation and litter management
- Reduce stress factors
- Proper brooding temperature
- Umbilical hygiene in hatchery

## 10. Salmonellosis (সালমোনেলোসিস)

### Overview
Caused by Salmonella bacteria. Multiple species affect poultry: S. Pullorum (Pullorum Disease/Bacillary White Diarrhea in chicks), S. Gallinarum (Fowl Typhoid in adults), and S. Enteritidis (food safety concern). Vertically transmitted (parent to egg).

### Symptoms
- **Pullorum Disease (chicks)**:
  - White pasty diarrhea (সাদা আটালো পায়খানা)
  - High mortality in first 2-3 weeks
  - Huddling near heat source
  - Pasted vents
- **Fowl Typhoid (adults)**:
  - Greenish-yellow diarrhea
  - Pale, shrunken comb
  - Drop in egg production
  - Enlarged liver and spleen (bronze liver at post-mortem)

### Treatment
- **Ciprofloxacin**: 10mg/kg BW for 5-7 days
- **Trimethoprim-Sulfamethoxazole**: 30mg/kg BW for 5-7 days
  - **Withdrawal period: 10 days for meat, 10 days for eggs**
- **Furazolidone**: 0.04% in feed for 10-14 days (where legal)
  - **Note**: Furazolidone is banned in some countries. Check DLS regulations.

### Prevention
- Source chicks from Salmonella-free parent flocks
- Whole Blood Agglutination test for breeders
- Maintain hygiene in hatchery and farm
- Acidify drinking water (pH 4-5 using organic acids)
- Competitive exclusion products (probiotics)

## 11. Aspergillosis / Brooder Pneumonia (ব্রুডার নিউমোনিয়া)

### Overview
Fungal infection caused by Aspergillus fumigatus. Common in chicks during brooding, especially when using damp or moldy litter.

### Symptoms
- Gasping in young chicks
- Rapid breathing
- Drowsiness
- Loss of appetite
- Increased thirst
- Greenish-yellow plaques in lungs and air sacs (post-mortem)
- Mortality usually 5-50% in chicks

### Treatment
- **Copper sulfate**: 1g per liter of drinking water for 3-5 days
- **Nystatin**: In feed at 100g per ton
- Improve ventilation
- Remove and replace damp litter
- **Withdrawal period for Copper sulfate: 0 days**

### Prevention
- Use fresh, dry litter material (rice husk preferred)
- Never use damp or moldy rice husk
- Maintain proper brooding temperature (32-35°C in first week)
- Good ventilation during brooding
- Disinfect shed with formalin fumigation before placing chicks

## Important Notes for Farmers

### When to Call a Veterinarian
- Sudden increase in mortality (>1% per day)
- More than 5% of flock showing symptoms simultaneously
- Respiratory symptoms spreading rapidly
- Sudden drop in egg production (>10% in one day)
- Bloody diarrhea in multiple birds
- Neurological signs (neck twisting, paralysis)
- Any suspected case of Avian Influenza

### Nearest Help
- **Upazila Livestock Office and Veterinary Hospital** (উপজেলা প্রাণিসম্পদ অফিস ও ভেটেরিনারি হাসপাতাল): Available in all 464 upazilas of Bangladesh
- Each office has at least 2 DLS officers (one veterinarian + one livestock officer)
- Mobile Veterinary Clinics available at district level for farm visits
- Report unusual mortality or suspected outbreaks to Regional Disease Investigation Laboratory (FDI)

### Antibiotic Usage Warning
- **Only 14.3% of farmers in Bangladesh know about antibiotic withdrawal periods**
- Always maintain withdrawal periods before selling meat or eggs
- Never use antibiotics as growth promoters
- Use antibiotics only when prescribed by a veterinarian
- Keep records of all medicines used
- Antibiotic residues in food are a public health concern
