# Poultry Vaccination Schedules for Bangladesh

## Broiler Vaccination Schedule

| Age | Vaccine | Disease | Route | Notes |
|-----|---------|---------|-------|-------|
| Day 1 (Hatchery) | Marek's Disease Vaccine (HVT) | Marek's Disease | Subcutaneous injection (neck) | Must be given within 24 hours of hatch |
| Day 1 (Hatchery) | IB (H120) | Infectious Bronchitis | Spray or eye drop | Optional; depends on farm risk |
| Day 3-5 | BCRDV (Baby Chick Ranikhet Disease Vaccine) | Newcastle Disease | 1 drop in eye | Bangla BCRDV — live lentogenic strain |
| Day 10-14 | IBD Vaccine (Gumboro) — Intermediate strain | Infectious Bursal Disease | Eye drop or drinking water | 1 drop per chick (eye) or dissolve in water |
| Day 18-21 | IBD Booster (Gumboro) | Infectious Bursal Disease | Drinking water | Second dose, especially in endemic areas |
| Day 21-24 | ND LaSota | Newcastle Disease | Eye drop or drinking water | Bangla ND LaSota — live lentogenic strain |
| Day 28 (optional) | IBD 3rd dose | Infectious Bursal Disease | Drinking water | Only in high-risk areas |

### Broiler Vaccination Notes
- Broilers have a short production cycle (35-42 days), so fewer vaccines are needed
- In Bangladesh, most broiler farms follow a 4-vaccine minimum: Marek's, BCRDV, Gumboro, LaSota
- Dissolve live vaccines in cool, clean, unchlorinated water
- Administer vaccines in the morning (cooler hours)
- Do not vaccinate sick birds
- Use vaccine within 1-2 hours of preparation

## Layer Vaccination Schedule

| Age | Vaccine | Disease | Route | Notes |
|-----|---------|---------|-------|-------|
| Day 1 (Hatchery) | Marek's Disease Vaccine (HVT) | Marek's Disease | Subcutaneous injection (neck) | Most critical vaccine — given at hatchery |
| Day 3-5 | BCRDV | Newcastle Disease | Eye drop (1 drop) | Baby Chick Ranikhet Disease Vaccine |
| Day 5-7 | IB (H120) | Infectious Bronchitis | Eye drop or spray | Can be combined with BCRDV timing |
| Day 10-14 | IBD Vaccine (Intermediate) | Gumboro Disease | Eye drop or drinking water | |
| Day 18-21 | IBD Booster | Gumboro Disease | Drinking water | |
| Day 21-24 | ND LaSota | Newcastle Disease | Eye drop or drinking water | |
| Week 4-6 | Fowl Pox Vaccine | Fowl Pox | Wing-web puncture | One dose gives lifelong immunity. Use before monsoon season |
| Week 6-8 | ND + IB Booster | Newcastle + Infectious Bronchitis | Eye drop or drinking water | Live vaccines |
| Week 6-8 | MG Vaccine | Mycoplasmosis (CRD) | Eye drop | Optional; for farms with history of MG |
| Week 8-10 | ND LaSota Booster | Newcastle Disease | Drinking water | |
| Week 10-12 | Infectious Coryza Vaccine | Infectious Coryza | Subcutaneous injection | For farms with history of coryza |
| Week 10-12 | Fowl Cholera Vaccine | Fowl Cholera | Subcutaneous injection | Optional; on farms with prior outbreaks |
| Week 12-14 | Avian Encephalomyelitis (AE) | Avian Encephalomyelitis | Drinking water | For breeders and layers, before egg production |
| Week 14-16 | ND + IB + EDS (Killed) | Newcastle, IB, Egg Drop Syndrome | Intramuscular or subcutaneous | Killed/inactivated trivalent vaccine — before laying |
| Week 16-18 | Salmonella Vaccine | Salmonellosis | Injection | Where required by regulation |
| Every 8-10 weeks during lay | ND LaSota | Newcastle Disease | Drinking water | Booster during production |

### Layer Vaccination Notes
- Layers have a long production cycle (up to 80 weeks), so comprehensive vaccination is essential
- Killed (inactivated) vaccines at 14-18 weeks provide long-lasting immunity during the laying period
- Record all vaccinations with date, batch number, and route
- Store vaccines at 2-8°C (refrigerator, NOT freezer for live vaccines)
- Check vaccine expiry date before use
- Wash hands before and after vaccination

## Sonali Chicken Vaccination Schedule

Sonali chickens (RIR × Fayoumi cross) are commonly raised in semi-scavenging systems in rural Bangladesh. A simplified vaccination schedule:

| Age | Vaccine | Disease | Route |
|-----|---------|---------|-------|
| Day 3-5 | BCRDV | Newcastle Disease | Eye drop |
| Day 14 | IBD Vaccine | Gumboro Disease | Eye drop or drinking water |
| Day 21 | IBD Booster | Gumboro Disease | Drinking water |
| Day 28 | ND LaSota | Newcastle Disease | Eye drop or drinking water |
| Week 5-6 | Fowl Pox | Fowl Pox | Wing-web puncture |
| Week 8-10 | ND LaSota Booster | Newcastle Disease | Drinking water |
| Week 16 (before laying) | Killed ND | Newcastle Disease | Injection |

### Sonali Notes
- Sonali are more disease-resistant than commercial breeds but still need vaccination
- Newcastle Disease is the biggest killer in rural/Sonali flocks
- Fowl Pox vaccination is especially important before monsoon (mosquito season)
- Many Sonali farmers skip vaccination — this is the main cause of losses

## Deshi (Local/Indigenous) Chicken Vaccination

For backyard/deshi chickens, a minimum vaccination program:

| Age | Vaccine | Disease | Route |
|-----|---------|---------|-------|
| Day 3-7 | BCRDV | Newcastle Disease | Eye drop |
| Day 28-35 | ND LaSota | Newcastle Disease | Eye drop or drinking water |
| Week 5-6 | Fowl Pox | Fowl Pox | Wing-web puncture |
| Every 3-4 months | ND LaSota | Newcastle Disease | Drinking water |

### Deshi Chicken Notes
- Newcastle Disease alone kills up to 60-80% of unvaccinated village flocks annually
- BCRDV is thermotolerant (heat-resistant) and specifically designed for rural Bangladesh
- Available from Upazila Livestock Offices at subsidized rates
- Community-based vaccination campaigns are conducted by DLS

## Vaccine Handling and Administration Guidelines

### Storage
- Live vaccines: Store at 2-8°C in refrigerator. **Never freeze.**
- Killed vaccines: Store at 2-8°C. **Never freeze.** If frozen, discard.
- Transport in ice box with ice packs during hot weather
- Protect from direct sunlight

### Water Administration Method
1. Withhold water for 1-2 hours before vaccination (not longer in hot weather)
2. Clean all drinkers thoroughly — no chlorine or disinfectant residue
3. Use cool, clean, unchlorinated water
4. Add skimmed milk powder (2g per liter) to protect vaccine virus
5. Dissolve vaccine in water
6. Provide enough drinkers so all birds can drink within 1-2 hours
7. Use prepared vaccine within 2 hours
8. Resume normal water supply after vaccination

### Eye Drop Method
1. Hold bird gently
2. Place 1 drop in one eye using the dropper provided
3. Wait for the bird to blink (ensures vaccine absorbs)
4. Release bird
5. This method ensures each bird receives an accurate dose

### Wing-Web Puncture (Fowl Pox)
1. Use the two-pronged needle provided with the vaccine
2. Dip needle into reconstituted vaccine
3. Pierce the wing web (avoid blood vessels and bone)
4. One puncture per bird
5. Check for "take" (swelling at site) after 7-10 days — indicates successful vaccination

### Common Vaccination Mistakes
- Vaccinating sick birds (wait until recovered)
- Using chlorinated water to dissolve vaccines
- Preparing vaccine too far in advance
- Not maintaining cold chain
- Skipping booster doses
- Using expired vaccines
- Incomplete flock coverage (missing birds)
