### FCR (Feed Conversion Ratio) Guide
- FCR = Total feed consumed / Total body weight gained
- Good FCR for broilers in Bangladesh: 1.5-1.7 at 35 days
- FCR above 2.0 indicates a problem (disease, feed quality, environment)
- **Lower FCR = More profitable**

### Broiler Feeding Tips for Bangladesh
- During hot weather (>35°C): feed during cooler hours (early morning 5-7 AM and evening 5-7 PM)
- Add Vitamin C (200-300mg/liter of water) during heat stress
- Increase energy in feed during hot weather (add vegetable oil 2-3%)
- Provide continuous access to cool, clean water
- Use pellet feed for better FCR
- Avoid dusty or moldy feed

### Calcium Management for Layers
- **Critical nutrient** for eggshell quality
- Layer feed must contain 3.5-4.0% calcium
- Provide oyster shell or limestone grit as free-choice supplement
- **2/3 of calcium should be in coarse/grit form** (limestone, oyster shell)
- Feed calcium supplement in the afternoon (eggshell forms at night)
- Signs of calcium deficiency: thin/soft shells, shell-less eggs, leg weakness, cage layer fatigue

## Sonali Chicken Feed Management

### Overview
Sonali chickens (RIR × Fayoumi cross) are raised in both semi-scavenging and intensive systems. They require less feed than commercial breeds and are more adaptable to local feed ingredients.

### Feeding Schedule

| Phase | Age | Crude Protein (%) | Feed/Bird/Day (grams) |
|-------|-----|-------------------|----------------------|
| Starter | 0-6 weeks | 20-21% | 15-45 |
| Grower | 6-16 weeks | 16-18% | 45-80 |
| Layer | 16+ weeks | 16-17% | 80-100 |

### Egg Production
- Sonali hens produce approximately **150-160 eggs per year** under good management
- Peak production: 60-70% (lower than commercial breeds)
- Egg weight: 40-48 grams (smaller than commercial breeds)
- Laying starts at 18-20 weeks of age

## Water Management

### Water is the Most Important Nutrient
- Chickens drink 1.5-2× the amount of feed they eat (by weight)
- In hot weather (>35°C), water consumption increases to 3-4× feed intake
- Water temperature should be below 25°C
- Dehydration kills faster than starvation

### Daily Water Requirement (per 1000 birds)

| Bird Type | Normal Weather (liters) | Hot Weather (liters) |
|-----------|------------------------|---------------------|
| Broiler (4 weeks) | 180-220 | 280-350 |
| Broiler (6 weeks) | 280-320 | 400-500 |
| Layer | 200-250 | 350-450 |

### Water Quality Parameters
| Parameter | Acceptable Level |
|-----------|-----------------|
| pH | 6.0-8.5 |
| Total Dissolved Solids | <1000 ppm |
| Hardness | <110 ppm |
| Chlorine (free) | 2-3 ppm |
| Iron | <0.3 ppm |
| Nitrate | <25 ppm |
| Coliform bacteria | 0 CFU/ml |

### Water Sanitation
- **Chlorinate water**: Add 2-3 ppm free chlorine at point of use
  - Use bleaching powder: 3g per 1000 liters of water
  - Or sodium hypochlorite (liquid bleach 5%): 6ml per 1000 liters
- Clean water lines and drinkers daily
- Flush water lines with hydrogen peroxide (3%) monthly
- Test water quality at least quarterly

## Common Feed Problems and Solutions

### Problem: Poor Growth / Low Body Weight
- **Check**: Feed quality, protein level, FCR
- **Solution**: Send feed sample for lab analysis; switch to higher protein feed; check for disease

### Problem: Soft/Thin Eggshells
- **Check**: Calcium level in feed, Vitamin D3 level
- **Solution**: Add oyster shell as supplement; ensure 3.5-4% calcium in feed; check Vitamin D3 (3000-3500 IU/kg)

### Problem: Drop in Egg Production
- **Check**: Feed intake (is it reduced?), light duration, water availability, disease, stress
- **Solution**: Ensure 16 hours of light for layers; check for disease; ensure feed intake is 100-120g/bird/day

### Problem: Feed Refusal / Reduced Intake
- **Check**: Feed freshness, mold, temperature
- **Solution**: In hot weather — feed during cool hours; add Vitamin C; check for mycotoxins; ensure fresh feed (don't store >2 weeks in hot weather)

### Problem: Wet / Sticky Droppings
- **Check**: Water quality, salt levels in feed, disease
- **Solution**: Check feed salt level (max 0.5%); test for coccidiosis; ensure water quality

### Problem: Feather Pecking / Cannibalism
- **Check**: Protein level, density, lighting, boredom
- **Solution**: Ensure adequate protein (especially methionine); reduce light intensity; reduce stocking density; provide enrichment

## Feed Storage Guidelines for Bangladesh Climate

### Hot and Humid Climate Challenges
- Bangladesh's hot and humid climate accelerates feed spoilage
- Aflatoxin contamination is a major risk (maize, groundnut cake)
- Rice polish becomes rancid quickly (use within 1 week in summer)

### Storage Best Practices
- Store feed in a cool, dry, well-ventilated room
- Keep bags off the floor on pallets or wooden slats (at least 15cm above ground)
- Maintain 30cm gap from walls for air circulation
- First-in, first-out (FIFO) rotation
- **Do not store feed for more than 2 weeks in summer** (3-4 weeks in winter)
- Inspect for mold, insects, and rodent damage regularly
- Use toxin binders in feed during monsoon season
- Keep feed store clean and rodent-proof
