# Poultry Breeds and Production Guide — Bangladesh

## Breed Categories in Bangladesh

### 1. Commercial Broiler Breeds

| Breed | Origin | Market Weight (35 days) | FCR | Notes |
|-------|--------|------------------------|-----|-------|
| Cobb 500 | USA | 1.9-2.1 kg | 1.55-1.65 | Most popular in Bangladesh |
| Ross 308 | UK/Scotland | 1.9-2.1 kg | 1.55-1.65 | Widely available |
| Hubbard | USA | 1.8-2.0 kg | 1.60-1.70 | Less common |
| Arbor Acres | USA | 1.8-2.0 kg | 1.60-1.70 | Available |

**Production characteristics (Cobb 500 / Ross 308)**:
- Day-old chick weight: 42-45g
- Market age: 32-35 days in Bangladesh
- Target weight at 35 days: 1.9-2.1 kg live weight
- Feed consumed to market: 3.0-3.5 kg
- Livability: 95-97%
- Dressing percentage: 68-72%

### 3. Sonali Chicken (সোনালী মুরগি)

**The most important dual-purpose breed in Bangladesh's poultry sector.**

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Origin | Cross of RIR male × Fayoumi female |
| Name meaning | "Golden" in Bangla (for golden feather color) |
| Introduction in BD | 1996-2000, northern Bangladesh |
| Egg production | 150-160 eggs/year |
| Age at first egg | 18-20 weeks |
| Egg weight | 40-48g |
| Adult body weight (male) | 2.0-2.5 kg |
| Adult body weight (female) | 1.5-1.8 kg |
| Meat color | Preferred golden-yellow skin |
| Day-old chick production (2010) | ~150.9 million (35% of BD total) |

**Advantages of Sonali**:
- Well adapted to Bangladesh's environment
- More disease-resistant than commercial breeds
- Requires less care and feed
- Suitable for semi-scavenging system
- Lower mortality than commercial breeds
- Meat and egg taste preferred by many consumers
- Can be raised by women and children in rural areas
- Lower investment cost

**Mortality rates by season**:
- Spring: 4.35% (lowest)
- Summer: 9.13%
- Rainy season: 10.15%
- Winter: 14.05% (highest — due to CRD)

**Farming systems for Sonali**:
1. **Semi-scavenging**: Birds scavenge during day, confined at night. Minimal supplementary feed.
2. **Semi-intensive**: Confined in shed with outdoor access. Regular feeding with locally available ingredients.
3. **Intensive**: Fully confined, commercial feed. Higher investment but better control.

### 4. Fayoumi (ফাউমি)

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Origin | Egypt |
| Egg production | 200-220 eggs/year (in intensive management) |
| Age at first egg | 16-18 weeks (early maturity) |
| Egg weight | 42-48g (small) |
| Adult weight | 1.3-1.7 kg |
| Egg production rate | ~36% (backyard conditions) |

**Characteristics**:
- Very hardy and disease-resistant
- Active, alert, and good forager
- Not broody (advantage for egg production)
- Early sexual maturity
- Low mortality
- Thrives in hot climate
- Small egg size is a disadvantage for commercial sale

### 5. RIR — Rhode Island Red (আরআইআর)

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Origin | USA |
| Egg production | 250-280 eggs/year (intensive) |
| Age at first egg | 20-22 weeks |
| Egg weight | 55-62g |
| Adult weight | 2.5-3.5 kg |

**Characteristics**:
- Dual-purpose (meat and egg)
- Larger eggs than Fayoumi or Sonali

- Good for intensive and semi-intensive systems

### 6. Deshi / Indigenous Chicken (দেশি মুরগি)

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Egg production | 50-80 eggs/year |
| Age at first egg | 22-26 weeks |
| Egg weight | 35-42g |
| Adult weight | 1.0-1.5 kg |
| Egg production rate | ~29% |
| Broodiness | Very high (major limitation) |

**Characteristics**:
- Highly disease-resistant
- Excellent scavengers
- Preferred meat taste and texture
- Very low input requirement
- High broodiness reduces laying time
- Slow growth
- Premium price in market (BDT 600-800 per bird)

## Egg Production Management

### Light Management for Layers

| Age | Light Duration | Intensity |
|-----|---------------|-----------|
| 0-2 weeks | 23-24 hours | Bright (30-40 lux) |
| 3-8 weeks | 16-18 hours | Medium (10-15 lux) |
| 9-16 weeks | 12-14 hours | Low (5-10 lux) |
| 17-18 weeks | 14 hours | Increasing (10-15 lux) |
| 19-20 weeks | 15 hours | 15-20 lux |
| 20+ weeks | 16-17 hours | 15-20 lux |

**Key rules**:
- **Never decrease light during laying period** — causes drop in production
- Increase light gradually (30 minutes per week) from week 17 onwards
- Maintain consistent light schedule
- Use timer for reliability
- In open-sided houses: supplement natural daylight with electric light
- During load-shedding: use battery/solar backup for light schedule

### Egg Collection and Handling

- Collect eggs **at least 3 times per day** (morning, midday, evening)
- In hot weather: collect 4-5 times to prevent quality loss
- Handle eggs gently to prevent hairline cracks
- Do not wash eggs (removes protective bloom/cuticle)
- Remove dirty or cracked eggs from sale batch
- Store in cool area (below 25°C) with small end down
- Use egg trays — do not stack loose eggs
- Market eggs within 3-5 days in hot weather

### Monitoring Egg Production

**Hen-Day Production (%) = (Eggs produced ÷ Hens alive that day) × 100**

| Performance Level | Hen-Day % | Action Needed |
|------------------|-----------|---------------|
| Excellent | >92% | Maintain current management |
| Good | 85-92% | Monitor, minor adjustments |
| Below Average | 75-85% | Investigate: feed, disease, stress, light |
| Poor | <75% | Urgent investigation: likely disease or major management failure |

### Common Causes of Egg Production Drop
1. **Disease**: Newcastle, IB, AI, Mycoplasmosis — most common cause
2. **Feed**: Low calcium, low protein, feed quality issues
3. **Water**: Shortage, contamination, high temperature
4. **Light**: Inconsistent or insufficient light program
5. **Stress**: Heat, overcrowding, predator disturbance, noise
6. **Age**: Natural decline after peak (30 weeks)
7. **Molting**: Natural or forced molt

## Broiler Production Management

### Brooding Management

| Parameter | Week 1 | Week 2 | Week 3 | Week 4+ |
|-----------|--------|--------|--------|---------|
| Temperature | 33-35°C | 30-32°C | 27-29°C | 24-26°C |
| Humidity | 60-70% | 60-65% | 55-60% | 50-60% |
| Light (hours) | 23-24 | 23 | 20-23 | 20-23 |
| Density (birds/sq ft) | 2.0 | 1.5 | 1.2 | 1.0 |

### Broiler Growth Monitoring
- Weigh sample of 30-50 birds weekly
- Compare against breed standard (Cobb/Ross guide)
- Uniform body weight is as important as average weight
- Coefficient of variation (CV) should be below 10%
- If average weight is 10% below standard, investigate:
  - Feed quality and intake
  - Water quality and access
  - Temperature management
  - Disease

### Harvest Timing and Economics
- Typical harvest age in Bangladesh: 32-35 days
- Target live weight: 1.8-2.2 kg
- Cost of production: approximately BDT 120-140 per kg live weight (varies with feed prices)
- Market price: BDT 160-190 per kg live weight (varies seasonally)
- Best profit when FCR is low and market price is high
- Keeping birds beyond optimal market age increases FCR and reduces profit margin
- Hot weather increases FCR by 0.1-0.2 points

## Market Price Context (Bangladesh)

### Government-Set Prices (Reference, subject to change)
| Product | Production Level (BDT) | Wholesale (BDT) | Retail (BDT) |
|---------|----------------------|-----------------|--------------|
| Broiler (per kg) | ~169 | ~173 | ~180 |
| Egg (per piece) | ~10 | ~11 | ~12 |

### Typical Market Prices (actual, as of 2025-2026)
- Broiler live weight: BDT 160-190 per kg
- Sonali/Deshi chicken: BDT 400-600 per kg (premium)
- Egg (commercial): BDT 10-14 per piece
- Sonali/Deshi egg: BDT 12-18 per piece (premium)

### Price Factors
- Feed prices (especially soybean meal, maize) drive production costs
- Seasonal demand: prices rise during Eid, Ramadan, winter wedding season
- Oversupply during peak production months can crash prices
- Government price controls are set but market often exceeds them

### Marketing Tips for Farmers
- Avoid selling all birds at once when market is low
- Track market prices before deciding harvest date
- Premium pricing for Sonali/Deshi birds — maintain breed identity
- Direct sales to local markets avoid middleman margins
- Record keeping helps calculate true profit per flock
