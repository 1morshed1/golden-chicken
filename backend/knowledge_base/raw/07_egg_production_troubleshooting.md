# Egg Production Troubleshooting Guide — Bangladesh

## Quick Diagnostic Table

| Problem | Most Likely Cause | Immediate Action |
|---------|-------------------|-----------------|
| Sudden drop (>10% in 1 day) | Disease (ND, IB, AI) | Isolate flock, contact vet, check vaccination history |
| Gradual decline | Age, nutrition, light | Check feed calcium, light hours, water access |
| No eggs from new flock | Too young, stress, underweight | Check age/weight, ensure 16hr light, reduce stress |
| Soft/thin shells | Low calcium, Vitamin D3 deficiency, IB | Add oyster shell, check feed calcium (3.5-4%), Vitamin D3 |
| Misshapen eggs | Infectious Bronchitis, stress, age | Test for IB, reduce stress |
| Blood spots in eggs | Genetics, Vitamin K deficiency, stress | Usually harmless; supplement Vitamin K if widespread |
| Dirty eggs | Nest box management, wet litter | Clean nests, fix wet litter, collect more frequently |
| Egg eating | Calcium deficiency, boredom, thin shells | Increase calcium, darken nest boxes, collect frequently |
| Double yolk eggs | Immature reproductive system (young birds) | Normal in first 4-6 weeks of lay, will resolve |
| Pale yolk color | Feed lacking pigment (xanthophyll) | Add maize/corn to feed, or commercial pigmenter |

## Detailed Troubleshooting

### 1. Sudden Production Drop

**Definition**: >5% drop in one day or >10% drop in one week

**Step-by-step investigation**:

1. **Check mortality**: Are birds dying? → Likely disease
   - Sudden deaths + respiratory signs → Newcastle Disease or Avian Influenza
   - **Action**: Contact Upazila Livestock Office immediately
   
2. **Check feed and water**:
   - Was there a feed change?
   - Did feed run out?
   - Is water available continuously?
   - Feed intake below 100g/bird/day indicates a problem
   
3. **Check environment**:
   - Was there a heat wave? (>35°C)
   - Was there a power outage (light disruption)?
   - Any construction noise, predator disturbance?
   - Stray dogs or foxes near shed at night?
   
4. **Check vaccination record**:
   - Is the flock up to date on ND, IB, EDS vaccines?
   - Were vaccines stored and administered correctly?

5. **Check for disease symptoms**:
   - Respiratory signs → IB, ND, CRD
   - Diarrhea → Various diseases
   - Swollen head/face → Coryza, AI
   - No visible symptoms but drop → Egg Drop Syndrome (EDS)

### 2. Poor Shell Quality

**Causes ranked by frequency in Bangladesh**:

1. **Low calcium in feed** (most common)
   - Solution: Ensure 3.5-4.0% calcium in layer feed
   - Add crushed oyster shell as free-choice supplement: 5-8g per bird per day
   - Feed oyster shell in the afternoon (shell formation happens at night)

2. **Vitamin D3 deficiency**
   - Vitamin D3 is needed for calcium absorption
   - Ensure 3000-3500 IU per kg feed
   - In enclosed houses: supplement Vitamin D3 in water

3. **Heat stress** 
   - Above 35°C: shell quality deteriorates (birds pant → lose CO₂ → alkalosis → poor calcification)
   - Solution: Cool the shed, add electrolytes, feed during cool hours

4. **Infectious Bronchitis**
   - IB damages the oviduct permanently in young birds
   - Results in rough, ridged, misshapen, or thin-shelled eggs
   - Prevention: vaccinate properly at correct age

5. **Age**
   - Shell quality naturally declines after 50 weeks
   - Increase calcium to 4.0-4.2% for older birds
   - Consider adding Vitamin C (100-200mg/kg feed)

6. **Mycotoxins in feed**
   - Aflatoxins interfere with calcium metabolism
   - Solution: use toxin binder, check feed quality

### 3. Egg Size Problems

**Small eggs**:
- Underdeveloped bird (underweight at point of lay)
- Solution: delay lighting program until birds reach target weight (1.4-1.5 kg for ISA Brown)
- Insufficient protein or methionine in feed
- Hot weather (reduces feed intake → smaller eggs)

**Large eggs (excessively)**:
- Overweight birds
- Excess protein in feed
- Can cause prolapse (vent blow-out) — serious welfare issue

### 4. Internal Egg Quality

**Watery albumen (thin egg white)**:
- Age-related (older birds)
- High ammonia in shed
- Disease (IB, ND)
- High storage temperature
- Solution: collect frequently, store cool, manage shed air quality

**Blood spots / meat spots**:
- Stress, Vitamin K deficiency
- Genetic predisposition
- Usually harmless to consumers
- Solution: Vitamin K supplement (2-3mg/kg feed), reduce stress

### 5. Production Not Starting on Time

**Expected start of lay**:
- Commercial layers (ISA Brown, Lohmann): 18-20 weeks
- Sonali: 18-20 weeks
- Fayoumi: 16-18 weeks
- Deshi: 22-26 weeks

**If delayed**:
1. Check body weight — birds may be underweight
   - ISA Brown should be ~1.45 kg at 18 weeks
   - Underweight birds delay laying; correct with increased feed quantity
2. Check light program — are birds getting 14-16 hours of light?
3. Check for disease — chronic CRD or coccidiosis can delay maturity
4. Check for stress — overcrowding, predators, excessive handling
5. Feed quality — protein too low during grower phase delays sexual maturity

## Seasonal Egg Production Tips for Bangladesh

### Summer (March-May)
- Egg production drops 5-15% due to heat stress
- Focus on cooling and water management
- Feed during cool hours
- Add Vitamin C and electrolytes
- Expect smaller eggs and thinner shells
- Collect eggs more frequently (4-5 times/day) to prevent quality loss

### Monsoon (June-September)
- Egg quality affected by high humidity
- Dirty eggs increase (wet litter)
- Maintain nest box cleanliness
- Watch for Fowl Pox (mosquito-transmitted) which reduces production
- Coccidiosis outbreaks can cause production drops
- Store eggs in coolest place available; sell quickly

### Winter (November-February)
- Best season for egg production in Bangladesh
- Egg production and shell quality improve
- Watch for respiratory diseases (CRD) which can drop production
- Maintain light program during shorter days
- Eggs store longer in cool weather

### Eid / Ramadan Season
- Higher demand and better prices
- Plan flock placement to have peak production during demand season
- Layer flocks should be 25-35 weeks old during peak demand
- Stock extra feed in case of supply disruption during holidays
