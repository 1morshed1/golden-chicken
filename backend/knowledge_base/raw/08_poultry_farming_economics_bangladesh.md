# Poultry Farming Economics — Bangladesh Context

## Industry Overview

Bangladesh has approximately:
- 8 grandparent stock farms
- ~100 breeder farms and hatcheries
- Over 70,000 commercial layer and broiler farms
- Millions of backyard/semi-scavenging poultry households
- Annual manure production: ~2.1 million tons
- ~150 million Sonali day-old chicks produced annually (as of 2010)

The poultry industry contributes significantly to GDP, employment, and rural livelihoods, especially for women in semi-scavenging systems.

### Revenue Calculation

- Livability: 95% = 950 birds survive
- Average live weight at 35 days: 2.0 kg
- Total live weight: 1,900 kg
- Sale price: BDT 170/kg (average)
- **Total revenue: BDT 323,000**

### Profit per Batch

| Scenario | Revenue | Cost | Profit | Per Bird |
|----------|---------|------|--------|---------|
| Good | 342,000 | 260,000 | 82,000 | BDT 82 |
| Average | 323,000 | 275,000 | 48,000 | BDT 48 |
| Poor | 285,000 | 290,000 | -5,000 | -BDT 5 |

### Key Profit Drivers
1. **Feed cost** — 70% of total cost. Even BDT 1/kg difference in feed price = BDT 3,500 per batch
2. **FCR** — Difference between 1.6 and 1.8 FCR = ~400 kg more feed = BDT 24,000 additional cost
3. **Mortality** — Each 1% increase = 10 birds lost = ~BDT 3,400 lost revenue
4. **Market price** — BDT 10/kg difference = BDT 19,000 per batch

### Number of Batches per Year
- Cycle: 35 days growing + 14-21 days rest = ~50-56 days
- Batches per year: 6-7 batches
- Annual profit potential (average): BDT 288,000-336,000 from 1000-bird shed

### Annual Revenue (1000 layers)

- Average Hen-Day production: 80% (conservative)
- Eggs per year: 1000 × 0.80 × 365 = 292,000 eggs
- Average egg price: BDT 11 per egg
- **Egg revenue: BDT 3,212,000**
- Spent hen sale (end of cycle): 900 birds × BDT 200 = BDT 180,000
- Manure sale: BDT 30,000-50,000 per year
- **Total annual revenue: ~BDT 3,422,000**

### Annual Profit
- Revenue: BDT 3,422,000
- Cost: BDT 2,762,000
- **Net Profit: ~BDT 660,000 per year** (average scenario)
- Per egg profit: ~BDT 2.3

### Break-Even Analysis
- Break-even egg price: ~BDT 9.5 per egg
- Break-even production rate: ~70% Hen-Day
- Below these levels: farm operates at a loss

- Lower feed cost (semi-scavenging possible)
- Premium meat and egg prices
- Lower mortality and medicine costs
- Lower infrastructure investment
- Growing consumer preference for "দেশি" type eggs/meat

## Cost Reduction Strategies

### Feed Cost Reduction (Biggest Impact)
1. **Buy feed in bulk** — negotiate volume discounts with feed mills
2. **Self-mix feed** — buy raw materials and mix on-farm (save 10-15%)
3. **Use locally available ingredients**: rice polish, broken rice, mustard oil cake, dried fish
4. **Optimize FCR** — better management = lower feed per unit of production
5. **Phase feeding** — match nutrient levels to bird's age/production stage
6. **Avoid feed wastage** — fill feeders only 1/3 full, use proper feeder height

### Mortality Reduction
1. **Vaccinate on time** — cheapest insurance against disease
2. **Maintain biosecurity** — prevent disease entry
3. **Good brooding management** — first week mortality determines batch success
4. **Clean water** — 80% of diseases enter through water
5. **Early detection** — daily observation catches problems early

### Revenue Optimization
1. **Track market prices** — sell at optimal time
2. **Reduce middlemen** — sell directly to local markets when possible
3. **Premium products** — Sonali/Deshi birds command 2-3× price of commercial broilers
4. **Manure sales** — properly composted poultry manure has good value for agriculture
5. **Contract farming** — reduces price risk (Aftab, Kazi, Paragon offer contracts)

## Government Support and Resources

### Department of Livestock Services (DLS)
- **Upazila Livestock Offices**: Available in all 464 upazilas
- Services: vaccination, disease diagnosis, training, subsidized medicines
- Mobile Veterinary Clinics at district level

### Credit and Loans
- Bangladesh Bank priority sector lending for poultry
- NGO microfinance (BRAC, Grameen, ASA) for small-scale poultry
- SME Foundation financing for medium-scale farms

### Training and Extension
- DLS conducts farmer training programs
- Bangladesh Poultry Industries Central Council (BPICC) provides industry coordination
- Universities (BAU, SAU, CVASU) offer research and advisory services

### Insurance
- Livestock insurance schemes exist but coverage is limited
- Discuss with local bank or insurance company for available products

## Common Financial Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Starting too large** — Begin with 500-1000 birds, learn, then expand
2. **No record keeping** — Without records, you can't calculate true profit
3. **Ignoring feed cost** — Feed is 70% of cost; a bad feed deal ruins profitability
4. **Skipping vaccination** — BDT 2-5 per bird for vaccination vs. potential total loss
5. **Single income source** — Diversify (eggs + meat + manure + composting)
6. **Borrowing at high interest** — Calculate if the farm can service the debt
7. **Not accounting for depreciation** — Shed and equipment wear out; plan for replacement
8. **Panic selling during market dip** — Results in unnecessary losses
9. **Overcrowding** — Short-term space saving leads to disease and lower performance
10. **No emergency fund** — Keep 2-3 months of operating expenses as reserve
